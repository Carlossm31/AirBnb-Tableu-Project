# 🏡 Airbnb Pricing & Revenue Analysis 

## 📊 Overview  
In this project, I built an interactive Tableau dashboard to analyze Airbnb listing prices, bedroom distribution, zipcode trends, and revenue patterns to uncover key business insights.


## 🖼️ Dashboard Preview  
![Airbnb Dashboard](https://github.com/Carlossm31/AirBnb-Tableu-Project/blob/536f81747028506bdafad8b7d17bdbab88892440/AirBNB%20Dashboard.png)


## 🎯 Objectives  
- Analyze how pricing varies by bedroom count  
- Identify high- and low-performing zip codes  
- Track revenue trends over time  
- Understand listing distribution across property types  


## 🛠️ Tools Used  
- Tableau (Data Visualization)  
- Excel / CSV (Data Cleaning & Preparation)


## 📌 Key Insights  

### Avg Price per Bedroom  
I analyzed how average listing price changes based on the number of bedrooms.  
- Prices increase significantly as bedroom count rises  
- 1-bedroom listings are the most affordable (~$96 avg)  
- 5–6 bedroom listings command premium pricing ($450–$585 range)  
- Indicates strong demand for larger group accommodations  

### Listing Distribution by Bedrooms  
I measured how listings are distributed across bedroom counts.  
- 1-bedroom listings dominate the market (1,800+ listings)  
- Listings decrease sharply as bedroom count increases  
- 5+ bedroom properties are limited in supply  

### Price per Zipcode (Map)  
I visualized geographic pricing differences.  
- Significant price variation across zip codes  
- Some areas exceed $150+ average price (premium locations)  
- Lower-priced regions indicate budget-friendly areas  

---

### Price by Zipcode (Bar Chart)  
I ranked zip codes based on average listing price.  
- Top zip codes exceed $200 average price  
- Lower-performing zip codes fall below ~$100  
- Highlights strong geographic pricing segmentation  


### Revenue Over Time  
I tracked revenue trends across the year.  
- Revenue grows steadily early in the year  
- Peaks around mid-year (~$2M+)  
- Remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations  
- Suggests seasonality in demand  



## 💡 Key Takeaways  
- Larger properties generate higher revenue per listing  
- Most inventory is concentrated in smaller units (1–2 bedrooms)  
- Location (zipcode) heavily impacts pricing  
- Demand appears seasonal with mid-year peaks
